1. Folic Acid Supplementation Protocol

Mechanism: Folic acid (vitamin B9) is essential for DNA methylation and transcription factor regulation, including genes like TBR1 involved in cortical development.

Dosing Guidelines:

  • Standard risk: 400-600 mcg/day (before conception through 12 weeks)
  • High risk (family history): 800-1000 mcg/day
  • Known TBR1 carrier: 1000-5000 mcg/day (under supervision)

Critical Windows:

⏰ Pre-conception to 12 weeks: Neural tube closure
⏰ 12-24 weeks: Cortical neurogenesis
⏰ 24-40 weeks: Synaptic development

🏥 Early Intervention Strategies

Evidence-based therapies for identified at-risk infants and toddlers

⏱️
Critical Period: Brain plasticity is highest 0-3 years. Early intervention during this window shows 60-70% improvement in developmental outcomes.

Tier 1: Behavioral Interventions (0-36 months)

Early Start Denver Model (ESDM)

Age: 12-48 months | Intensity: 15-25 hours/week

Evidence: RCT showed 15-point IQ gain, improved language (Level I)

Naturalistic Developmental Behavioral Interventions (NDBI)

Age: 6-36 months | Intensity: 10-20 hours/week

Evidence: Meta-analysis: moderate-large effects on social communication
